Visual Style

Usability

Adhere to standards and be consistent

Adhering to standards for interface elements and interactions helps users to be able to intuitively use your interface. This is because they can build upon existing knowledge.

Even experienced and tech savvy users benefit from standards: They, too, learn and adopt it better and they feel more save when doing critical actions.

Show what you can do and how

If you signify possible interactions you don't leave people wondering how they can get to their goal. A button, for example, is a signifier which shows that an area is clickable or tapable and that some acti on will be executed after clicking it.

To leave out such signifiers may be tempting due to saving space, adding functions without changing the UI or keeping it visually clean. However, it means that you leave your users guessing what the UI does.

Feedback on what the stuff does

If nothing happens after a click, users will wonder if their actions actually were noted.

Do user tests
